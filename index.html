🧠 Study Tips That Actually Work
▼
🧪 Active Recall
Don't just re-read — quiz yourself! That's why this app works. Testing yourself is 3x more effective than re-reading notes. Close your notes and try to remember first.
📅 Spaced Repetition
Study a little each day instead of cramming. Your brain needs sleep to move info to long-term memory. Even 15 min today + 15 min tomorrow beats 1 hour all at once.
📦 Chunking
Group related stuff together. The Civil War Amendments (13-14-15) go together: Free → Citizen → Vote. The "Court Cases about Rights" group together. Chunks are easier to remember!
🏠 Memory Palace
Imagine walking through your house. Put each amendment in a different room. The weirder the image, the better you'll remember it! Put the 1st Amendment at your front door, 2nd in the kitchen, etc.
🔤 First Letter Mnemonics
Take the first letters of things you need to remember and make a word or sentence. Like RAPPS for the 1st Amendment rights (Religion, Assembly, Press, Petition, Speech)!
🗣 Teach Someone
If you can explain it to your mom, dad, or friend without looking, you KNOW it. Teaching forces your brain to organize what it knows. Try explaining judicial review right now!
