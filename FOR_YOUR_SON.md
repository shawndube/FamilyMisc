## 🎮 Get Started in 30 Seconds

1. **Open the app** in your browser (index.html file)
2. **Tap "LEARN"** at the bottom (it should be the first tab)
3. **Pick something** that sounds interesting (14th Amendment? Separation of Powers?)
4. **Read the story** - Don't try to memorize, just understand what happened
5. **Done!** Move to the next one or take a break

That's it. No complicated setup. No timer. No scoring. Just learning.

## 📖 What You're Going to See

When you click on a concept, you'll see:

### The Big Idea

One sentence explaining why this thing exists. Just the core.

### The Story

The history of it. WHY was it created? What problem did it solve? This is the most important part - read this carefully.

### What It Actually Does

What this law/concept does RIGHT NOW. Real, concrete actions.

### Real-World Example

How does this affect YOUR life, today? Examples use modern stuff (TikTok, Instagram, phones) so you'll actually care.

### Connections

How does this relate to other concepts? Helps your brain see the big picture.

## 🎯 Study Tips That Actually Work

### Don't Memorize - Understand

- Don't try to memorize the exact wording
- Just understand the STORY
- When you understand WHY something exists, you automatically remember it

### Read, Then Explain

- Read a concept
- Close the app
- Explain it to your parent/friend/yourself
- If you have to look at the app again, read it once more then try explaining
- When you can explain it without looking = you know it

### Study in Small Chunks

- 15-20 minutes per concept, not an hour marathon
- Your brain gets tired. Short sessions work better.
- It's like video games - better to play 20 min and come back than grind for 2 hours straight

### Use Timeline to Connect Things

- Don't just learn amendments in random order
- Look at the Timeline to see WHEN things happened
- See how everything connects chronologically
- This helps your brain make sense of it

### Jump Around

- You don't have to learn amendments 1-27 in order
- Pick something interesting: "26th Amendment" (you can vote at 18!), "Miranda v. Arizona" (cops have to read you rights), "Tinker v. Des Moines" (you have free speech at school)
- When you're interested, you learn better

## 💡 Key Concepts to Start With

These are gateway concepts. Learn these first, then everything else clicks:

### CORE IDEAS (Start here!)

1. **Separation of Powers** - Government split 3 ways (Congress, President, Courts)
2. **Checks and Balances** - Each can stop the others from getting too powerful
3. **Federalism** - Federal government + state governments sharing power

Once you know these 3, everything else makes sense.

### THE BIG THREE AMENDMENTS

4. **13th Amendment** - Slavery ended
5. **14th Amendment** - Rights and citizenship for everyone
6. **15th Amendment** - Can't stop people voting based on race

These three changed EVERYTHING after the Civil War.

### THE IMPORTANT MODERN ONES

7. **1st Amendment** - Free speech, religion, press, protest
8. **26th Amendment** - You can vote at 18!

## 🤔 If You Get Stuck

### "I don't understand this concept"

→ Read the STORY again (stories explain better than definitions)
→ Look at the REAL WORLD EXAMPLE
→ Ask why it matters (that usually clicks it)

### "This is boring"

→ Pick a different concept (go back and choose something more interesting)
→ Jump to the Timeline to see when it happened
→ Read the court case stories instead (they're like mini dramas)

### "I forgot what I learned yesterday"

→ That's NORMAL (your brain needs time to process)
→ Read it again - second time will feel easier
→ Explain it to someone else
→ Check the Timeline to remember the context

### "This is too much"

→ You don't need to learn everything today
→ Pick 5 key concepts and master those first
→ Rest doesn't disappear

## ⚡ Pro Tips for ADHD Brain Success

1. **Brain needs VARIETY** - Don't study amendments 1-10 straight. Mix: Core idea → Amendment → Court case → Timeline

2. **YOU NEED BREAKS** - 15-20 min study, 5 min break. That's the magic combo. Marathon studying = worse learning

3. **MOVEMENT HELPS** - Study for 15 min, then move around, come back. Walking while thinking about a concept? Even better.

4. **TALK ABOUT IT** - Explaining to someone else is the BEST way to learn. Talk to your parent, a friend, even a pet. You'll remember it better.

5. **DRAW/DOODLE** - Draw a timeline while reading. Doodle what you're learning. It locks it in.

6. **CONNECTIONS ARE KEY** - Your ADHD brain LOVES patterns. "Oh, this connects to that!" moments are when learning sticks.
